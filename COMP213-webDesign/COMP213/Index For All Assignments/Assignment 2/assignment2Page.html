Information and Resources about Protected Areas

Protected Areas

A protected area is a clearly defined geographical space, recognised, dedicated and managed, through legal or other effective means, to achieve the long term conservation of nature with associated ecosystem services and cultural values.

Why do we protect these areas?

Protected areas – national parks, wilderness areas, community conserved areas, nature reserves are all protected areas , simply because these areas provide for us humans in a local area, conserving nature and the services it provides us such as food, clean water supply, medicines and protection from the impacts of natural disasters.

Who/What is trying to help?

A big player in protecting these areas is the World Commission on Protected Areas' IUCN Programme implemented by 6 Commissions

The IUCN Programme provides the framework for the conservation work undertaken by the Commissions and the Secretariat with and on behalf of IUCN Members.
